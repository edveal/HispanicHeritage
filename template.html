Oral Histories


The Houston Oral History Project is an effort to record and preserve the dynamic history of Houston through the stories and experiences of its residents. The Project includes HMRC’s Oral History Collection as well as the Mayor Bill White Collection and Neighborhood Voices. Within this collection of over a thousand interviews are the many voices of Houston’s Hispanic community.

In addition, the Houston Metropolitan Research Center often partners with local institutions and scholars to create Oral History Projects that fill a gap in Houston’s historical record. Below are selected oral history projects related to Houston’s Hispanic history.

Latino Veterans of WWII Oral History Project

In recognition of the 70th Anniversary of the end of World War II Mikaela Selley, Hispanic Collections Archivist at the Houston Metropolitan Research Center, and Dr. Jesus J. Esparza, Professor of History at Texas Southern University and Houston Community College, began the Latino Veterans Oral History Project in February of 2015. In a span of five months, 12 interviews were conducted with local Hispanic veterans of the Second World War. Each interview was filmed at the veterans’ home and is approximately one to two hours in length. The interviews captured biographical histories and the veteran experiences before during and after the war. The interviews are permanently housed as part of the Houston Metropolitan Research Center’s Oral History Collection.
